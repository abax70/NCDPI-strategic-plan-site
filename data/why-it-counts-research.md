# Why It Counts — Research Citations

## ACT Composite Score
- **ACT Inc., "Predicting Long-Term College Success through Degree Completion Using ACT Composite Score," 2012.** Using 190,000+ students, ACT Composite score is a significant independent predictor of first-year college GPA and degree completion within six years.
- **Mattern & Cruce, "The Positive Relationship between ACT Score and Future Annual Earnings," ACT Data Byte, 2021.** Tracking 9.1 million ACT-tested individuals, higher ACT scores are consistently associated with higher earnings by age 28.
- **Allensworth & Clark, "High School GPAs and ACT Scores as Predictors of College Completion," Educational Researcher, 2020.** ACT provides a common yardstick across schools, helping identify readiness for college-level work regardless of where a student attends.

## Advanced Placement (AP)
- **Smith, Hurwitz & Avery, "Giving College Credit Where It Is Due," Journal of Labor Economics, 2017.** Rigorous regression discontinuity on 4.5M students: earning a credit-granting AP score (3+) causally increases probability of bachelor's degree within four years by 1-2 percentage points per exam.
- **College Board, "Impacts of AP: More Than a Score."** Even students who score below 3 show better college outcomes than similar non-AP peers. Participation itself builds college-ready academic skills.
- **Warne, "Research on the Academic Benefits of the AP Program," SAGE Open, 2017.** Access to AP opens the door, but the research-backed payoff comes primarily from taking and passing the exam.

## Dual Enrollment
- **CCRC, "The Postsecondary Outcomes of High School Dual Enrollment Students," 2024.** 81% of dual enrollment students enrolled in college in the first year after HS (vs. ~70% overall). Completers earned degrees at higher rates across 41 states.
- **IES What Works Clearinghouse, "Dual Enrollment Programs," 2017.** Based on 5 rigorous studies (77,249 students): positive effects on degree attainment, college access, credit accumulation, and HS completion.
- **NCES, "Dual Enrollment: Participation and Characteristics," 2019.** Students can shorten time to degree, reduce tuition costs, and lower student loan debt.

## Career and Technical Education (CTE)
- **ACTE, "CTE Works!" 2025 + NCES.** Average graduation rate for CTE concentrators is 96%, vs. 85% nationally.
- **CTE Research Network / AIR, "What We Know About the Impact of CTE," 2024.** Systematic review of 28 causal studies: CTE has positive effects on HS achievement, completion, employability, and college readiness. No negative effects found.
- **Georgetown CEW, "Career and Technical Education: Five Ways That Pay."** 29 million U.S. jobs pay $35,000+ without a bachelor's degree; CTE pathways are viable routes to middle-class earnings.
- **Fordham Institute, "How Attaining Industry-Recognized Credentials in HS Shapes Outcomes," 2024.** Earning industry credentials in HS is associated with positive gains in employment; some fields also boost college enrollment.

## NAEP Reading
- **Annie E. Casey Foundation, "Early Warning! Why Reading by the End of Third Grade Matters," 2010.** Children not reading proficiently by end of 3rd grade are 4x more likely to not graduate.
- **Hernandez, "Double Jeopardy," Annie E. Casey Foundation, 2011.** 23% of below-basic 3rd-grade readers dropped out, vs. 4% of proficient readers.
- **Chall, "Stages of Reading Development."** Critical shift at grades 3-4: "learning to read" to "reading to learn." Students who haven't mastered fluent decoding by this transition struggle across all content areas.
- **Grade differentiation: YES.** G4 = foundational decoding, "learning to read" transition, dropout risk. G8 = content-area literacy, reading to learn across subjects, high school readiness.

## NAEP Math
- **Duncan et al., "School Readiness and Later Achievement," Developmental Psychology, 2007.** Meta-analysis of 6 longitudinal datasets: early math skills are the single strongest predictor of later academic achievement, stronger than early reading.
- **Siegler et al., "Early Predictors of High School Mathematics Achievement," Psychological Science, 2012.** 5th-graders' fractions/division knowledge uniquely predicted algebra and overall math achievement in HS.
- **Ritchie & Bates, "Enduring Links From Childhood Mathematics and Reading Achievement to Adult SES," Psychological Science, 2013.** Math ability at age 7 predicted income and job attainment at age 42, controlling for childhood SES and IQ.
- **Grade differentiation: YES (moderate).** G4 = foundational skills (number sense, fractions) as strongest predictor of all later achievement. G8 = gateway to algebra, calculus, STEM pipeline, and college/career access.

## Schools of Character
- **Jeynes, "A Meta-Analysis on Character Education and Student Achievement," Education and Urban Society, 2019.** Across 52 studies: character education associated with higher academic achievement and positive behavioral outcomes.
- **Durlak et al., "The Impact of Enhancing Students' SEL," Child Development, 2011.** Meta-analysis of 213 programs (270,034 students): 11-percentile-point gain in academic achievement; $11 return per $1 invested.
- **Berkowitz & Bier, "What Works in Character Education," 2005.** 33 programs with rigorous evidence improved academic, behavioral, and social-emotional outcomes.

## Public School Enrollment
- **Learning Policy Institute, "Community Schools as an Effective School Improvement Strategy," 2017.** Well-implemented community schools improve attendance, achievement, and graduation — up to $15 in social value per $1 spent.
- **Century Foundation, "How Racially Diverse Schools and Classrooms Can Benefit All Students," 2016.** Students in diverse schools show stronger academic outcomes; adults who attended integrated schools had higher earnings and better health.
- **NCES, "Condition of Education" indicators, 2024.** Public schools serve 49.6M students; 91% participate in USDA meal programs; IDEA mandates special education services at no cost to families.
