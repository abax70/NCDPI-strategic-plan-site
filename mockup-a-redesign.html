Why Does This Measure Matter?

Graduation rate is the most widely recognized measure of a public school system's success. Every student who earns a diploma gains access to more career opportunities, higher lifetime earnings, and better health outcomes. North Carolina's rate has been climbing steadily — and reaching 92% by 2030 would put us among the top states in the nation.

How Is NC Performing?

North Carolina's four-year cohort graduation rate reached a record high of 87.7% in 2024–25, up from 87.0% the prior year. The state has set annual targets that ramp toward the 92% goal by 2030.

Measure definition: The cohort graduation rate measures the percentage of 9th-graders who go on to graduate from high school within four years.

Next update: Data for the next cohort will be available September 2026.
