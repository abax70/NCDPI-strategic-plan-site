Axis Treatment Options — Cohort Graduation Rate

Three ways to show both the level AND the movement on Best-in-Nation measures.
The problem: Strategic-plan targets are intentionally incremental. On a 0–100% graduation scale, a jump from 87.7% (today) to 92% (2030 goal) is a ~4-point change that visually looks flat on a zero-anchored bar. But we cannot truncate the y-axis on a bar chart without misleading viewers. All three options below keep a zero-anchored bar as the anchor and add a second view to communicate the movement honestly.
Option 1
Stacked trajectory
Zero-anchored bar on top (magnitude) + a small dot-and-line trajectory below with a clearly-labeled above-zero range (movement).

Shared design decisions across all three:

• The top (zero-anchored) bar chart is unchanged from the current dashboard — same navy fill, same hatched target bars, same 2030 target reference line.

• Years shown: 2025 (current) through 2030 (goal). The 2024 baseline is omitted per your direction; we can add it back if needed.

• For the zoomed trajectory (Options 1 & 2): y-axis range is floor(min − 10% of baseline-to-target gap) to ceil(target + 5% of gap) — a consistent rule, not cherry-picked per measure.

• For the re-centered progress bar (Option 3): x-axis runs from the earliest baseline to target + 1 unit, so the 2030 goal sits near the right edge.
